---
title: Azure Kill Chain
description: The Azure Kill Chain is a framework that helps organizations understand the stages of a cyber attack on Azure.
---

## **I. Recon & Discovery**
Start by gathering information using **OSINT** and **unauthenticated techniques**. Learn to validate email IDs, discover the services a company is using in Azure, and understand the default permissions available to an Azure AD user.

## **II. Initial Access**
This is where attackers get in! From **password spraying** to **phishing**, attackers exploit weak points to gain initial access. They may also exploit **CI/CD pipelines** or **Managed Identity** to infiltrate Azure systems and gain a foothold.

## **III. Enumeration**
Once inside, attackers gather information. They enumerate **users, groups, applications**, and resources like **VMs, storage accounts**, and **key vaults**. The goal is to map attack paths and identify weak points using tools like **ROADRecon** and **Azure Hound**.

## **IV. Privilege Escalation**
Attackers escalate their privileges by exploiting weaknesses in services like **Automation Accounts**, **Key Vaults**, and **Custom Roles**. They also leverage **RunCommand** and **Custom Script Extensions** to run commands on Azure VMs, gaining further control.

## **V. Lateral Movement**
After escalating privileges, attackers move laterally—both within **Azure** and between **on-premises** and **cloud** environments. They exploit hybrid setups, **GitHub actions**, and **Pass-The-PRT** attacks to access more resources.

## **VI. Persistence**
Once inside, attackers look for ways to stay. By exploiting features like **SSO**, **Azure AD Connect**, and **Golden SAML**, they ensure they can maintain access to the environment, even if their initial point of entry is discovered.

## **VII. Data Mining**
Attackers search for valuable secrets. This includes extracting sensitive data from **Key Vaults**, **blob storage**, and **compromised workstations**. They also mine **deployment history** and **role assignments** for additional insights.

## **VIII. Defenses Bypass**
Attackers often bypass defenses. They practice ways to evade detection from **Azure AD Identity Protection** and **Conditional Access Policies**. They also find ways to bypass **MFA** and cloud protections like **NSGs** and **Azure Firewalls** to access critical systems.
