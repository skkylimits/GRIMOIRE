**B2B collaboration** in Microsoft Entra External ID lets you securely invite and work with external users, sharing apps and services while keeping control of your company data—even if partners don’t use Entra ID or have IT teams, using their own identities—corporate, government, or social accounts..

* Support employee collaboration with partners and guests via B2B invitations or self-service sign-up in your main tenant.

> It’s a flexible solution for both app developers and businesses needing secure external access.

## When

### Collaborate With Any Partner Using Their Identities

With Microsoft Entra B2B, partners use their own identities to access your apps, so you don’t manage external accounts or passwords. It supports work, school, or social logins, reducing admin overhead.

**Collaboration controls:**

* **Cross-tenant access settings**: Manage B2B access with other Entra orgs, trust their MFA and device claims, and set org-specific or default rules.
* **External collaboration settings**: Control who can invite guests, block domains, and limit guest access.
* **Microsoft cloud settings**: Enable B2B collaboration across different Microsoft clouds (e.g., global, government, 21Vianet).

### Easily Invite Guest Users From The Azure Portal

As an admin, you can quickly add guest users using the Azure portal:

1. **[Create guest user](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/b2b-quickstart-add-guest-users-portal)**
2. **Assign access**
3. **[Send invitation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/invitation-email-elements)**
4. **User signs in**

![review-permissions](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/review-permissions.png)

### Allow Self-service Sign-up

[Create a sign-up experience](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/self-service-sign-up-overview) for external users to access your apps:

* Let users sign up with social or enterprise identity providers.
* Collect user info during the sign-up process.
* Use API connectors to:
  * Integrate with external systems
  * Add custom approvals
  * Verify identities and user info.

![sign-up-sign-in-page](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/sign-up-sign-in-page.png)

### Use Policies To Securely Share Your Apps And Services

You can use authentication and authorization policies to protect your corporate content. Conditional Access policies, such as multifactor authentication, can be enforced:

- At the tenant level.
- At the application level.
- For specific guest users to protect corporate apps and data.

![get-started](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/get-started.png)

### Let Application And Group Owners Manage Their Own Guest Users

You can delegate guest user management to application owners so that they can add guest users directly to any application they want to share, whether it's a Microsoft application or not.

- Administrators set up self-service app and group management.
- Non-administrators use their [Access Panel](https://myapps.microsoft.com/) to add guest users to applications or groups.

![self-service-application-portal](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/self-service-application-portal.png)

### Customize The Onboarding Experience Fr B2B Guest Users

Bring your external partners on board in ways customized to your organization's needs.

- Use [Microsoft Entra entitlement management](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/id-governance/entitlement-management-overview) to configure policies that [manage access for external users](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/id-governance/entitlement-management-external-users#how-access-works-for-external-users).
- Use the [B2B collaboration invitation APIs](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/api/resources/invitation) to customize your onboarding experiences.

### Integrate with IdP

**Microsoft Entra External ID** lets users sign in with existing accounts (like Facebook, Google, Microsoft, or enterprise IDs) by setting up federation with [external identity providers](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/identity-providers)—no need to create new accounts just for your app.

![integrate-with-identity-providers](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/integrate-with-identity-providers.png)

### Integrate With SharePoint & OneDrive

You can [integrate SharePoint and OneDrive](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/sharepoint/sharepoint-azureb2b-integration) with Azure B2B to securely share content with external users. Guests use the same permissions as internal users.

- Enable [email one-time passcodes](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/one-time-passcode) as a backup sign-in method.

![email-one-time-passcode-guests](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/7.external-identities/email-one-time-passcode-guests.png)

### Apps For Consumers And Business Customers

Organizations and developers can use Microsoft Entra External ID as a CIAM solution in a separate external tenant to manage apps and users separately from their workforce. Features include:

* Custom self-service sign-up flows with options like email/password, one-time passcodes, or social accounts (Google, Facebook).
* Custom branding of sign-in pages with logos, colors, and images.
* Collecting user info with built-in or custom attributes.
* Analyzing user activity for insights and growth.

Customers sign in with existing identities, while benefiting from Microsoft Entra’s security, compliance, and scalability.

### Collaborate With Business Guests

Microsoft Entra External ID B2B collaboration lets your workforce securely work with external partners using their own credentials—no new passwords needed. Guests access your Office 365, SaaS, and business apps after their home identity is verified.

Ways to add guests:

* Invite users via Microsoft Entra, Microsoft accounts, or social IDs like Google, with simple sign-in.
* Use self-service sign-up flows for guests to register themselves, customizable with work, school, or social accounts.
* Manage access at scale with Microsoft Entra entitlement management, automating requests, reviews, and expirations.

::note
Guest user accounts live in your directory, can be managed like employees, and assigned permissions. Use cross-tenant access and external collaboration settings to control collaboration across organizations and clouds.
::

### External Vs Workforce Tenants

A tenant is a secure Microsoft Entra ID instance holding an organization’s users and apps. There are two types:

* **Workforce tenant:** Contains employees, internal apps, and resources. Supports collaboration with external partners via B2B.
* **External tenant:** Used for consumer or business customer apps. Holds app registrations and customer accounts, separate from workforce data.

### Feature Sets

| Thing              | Workforce Tenant (B2B)              | External Tenant (B2C-like)          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Who it's for       | Business partners, vendors          | App users, customers, consumers     |
| Where users live   | Same tenant as your employees       | Separate external tenant            |
| Sign-in experience | Microsoft-style, customizable       | Fully custom, no Microsoft branding |
| SSO support        | Works with M365, SaaS, on-prem apps | Only for your own registered apps   |
| Branding           | Can add your logo                   | Fully custom branding per app       |
| Best for           | Collaboration                       | Customer-facing apps                |

### B2B Direct Connect

**B2B Direct Connect**(Microsoft Entra) creates a **two-way trust** between organizations, enabling **Teams shared channels** without adding users as guests.

* Users stay in their home org but access shared channels seamlessly (chat, files, apps) without switching accounts.
* Channel owners can find and add external users from trusted orgs.
* Access is controlled through **cross-tenant policies**.
* Billed per **monthly active user (MAU)**.

Perfect for secure, frictionless collaboration between orgs in Teams.

### Azure AD B2C (Legacy)

**Azure AD B2C** is Microsoft’s older solution for **customer identity and access management**. It uses a **separate consumer directory**, managed via the Azure portal, and supports **customizable user journeys** through the Identity Experience Framework.

* Separate from Microsoft Entra ID
* Highly customizable sign-in/sign-up flows
* Managed via Azure AD B2C-specific portal

For details, check out [Azure AD B2C documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/active-directory-b2c/).

### Entra Entitlement Management for Guest Sign-Up

Let **external users self-sign-up** for access to your resources using **[Entra entitlement management](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/id-governance/entitlement-management-overview)**. You define [access policies](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/id-governance/entitlement-management-external-users#how-access-works-for-external-users), and upon approval, guests are automatically:

* Provisioned with accounts
* Assigned to groups, apps, and SharePoint sites

Ideal when you don’t know who the external users will be in advance.

### Microsoft Entra Graph API for B2B

Use **Microsoft Graph APIs** to manage B2B collaboration:

* **[Cross-tenant access API](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/api/resources/crosstenantaccesspolicy-overview?view=graph-rest-beta&preserve-view=true)**: Automate inbound/outbound policy setup, manage org/group/user/app access, and accept MFA/device claims.
* **[Invitation Manager API](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/api/resources/invitation)**: Build custom guest onboarding, send invites, or use `inviteRedeemUrl` for tailored user experiences.

### CA for B2B

Apply [Conditional Access policies to B2B](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/external-id/authentication-conditional-access) and direct connect users like internal users. If MFA or device compliance is required, you can **trust claims from the user's home org**, enabling seamless sign-on if conditions are already met—otherwise, the user is challenged in their own tenant.

![cross-tenant-auth](/content/7.knowledge-base/4.operating-systems/5.entra-id/1.identity/8.external-identities/cross-tenant-auth.png)

1. A Fabrikam user signs in to a resource in Contoso.
2. Contoso’s Conditional Access and cross-tenant access settings (Fabrikam’s outbound and Contoso’s inbound) are evaluated.
3. Contoso checks if it trusts MFA and device claims from Fabrikam; if not, skip to step 6.
4. If trusted, Microsoft Entra verifies the user’s MFA and device claims from Fabrikam’s session.
5. If MFA or device claims are missing, challenges are issued in Fabrikam; access is granted only if requirements are met.
6. Without trust settings, B2B collaboration users must complete MFA in Contoso; B2B direct connect users are blocked if MFA or device compliance can’t be verified.

### Multitenant Applications

A [multitenant org](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/identity/multi-tenant-organizations/) uses multiple Microsoft Entra ID instances—often for cloud or regional needs. **[Cross-tenant synchronization](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/identity/multi-tenant-organizations/cross-tenant-synchronization-overview)** enables seamless access across tenants without invites or consent prompts. Settings are managed via *Organization-specific access*.
