**Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA)** is a security model that assumes no implicit trust, whether inside or outside the corporate network. It mandates continuous verification of all users and devices attempting to access resources, regardless of their location or network status. 

The core principle of ZTNA is “never trust, always verify,” ensuring that access is granted based on strict authentication and contextual factors.

## Key Principles:

### 1. **Identity and Access Management**

Enforces strict identity verification through multi-factor authentication (MFA) and continuous monitoring of user and device identities.

### 2. **Least Privilege Access**

Grants access based on the minimum necessary permissions required for a specific task, reducing the potential impact of a breach.

### 3. **Contextual Access Control**

Considers various factors such as user role, device health, location, and behavior to make dynamic access decisions.

### 4. **Micro-Segmentation**

Divides the network into smaller, isolated segments to limit lateral movement within the network and contain potential breaches.

### 5. **Continuous Monitoring**

Continuously assesses and verifies the security posture of users and devices, ensuring ongoing compliance with access policies.

### 6. **Policy Enforcement**

Applies security policies consistently across all access requests, regardless of whether the request comes from inside or outside the network.
