**Definition**: A user authentication process that allows individuals to access multiple applications or systems with a single set of credentials.

## **Key Aspects**

1. **Authentication Process**:
   - **How It Works**: SSO allows users to authenticate once with a central Identity Provider (IdP), which then grants access to multiple applications without requiring additional logins.

2. **Integration Points**:
   - **Integration with Applications**: SSO can be integrated with various on-premises or cloud-based applications, providing seamless access across an organization’s digital ecosystem.
   - **Identity Providers**: SSO relies on IdPs like **Okta**, **Azure Active Directory (AD)**, or **Google Workspace** to manage and verify credentials.

3. **Security Considerations**:
   - **Token Management**: Authentication tokens are securely managed and stored by the IdP, ensuring that access remains secure.
   - **Single Point of Failure**: While convenient, SSO requires robust security controls to protect against potential vulnerabilities, as a compromised IdP could provide unauthorized access to multiple systems.
