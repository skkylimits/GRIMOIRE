**Access Review** is a systematic process of evaluating and verifying user access permissions to ensure they are appropriate, up-to-date, and aligned with organizational policies and security requirements. This ongoing evaluation helps prevent unauthorized access, maintain compliance, and enhance the overall security posture of the organization.

## **Key Concepts**

1. **Definition**: Access Review involves periodic checks of user access rights to verify that permissions align with current roles and responsibilities. This process ensures that users have the appropriate level of access based on their job functions and that obsolete or excessive permissions are promptly revoked.

2. **Frequency**: Reviews are conducted at regular intervals—such as quarterly, biannually, or annually—depending on organizational policies and regulatory requirements. The frequency may vary based on the sensitivity of the data and the criticality of the systems involved.

3. **Process**: The review process typically includes:

   - **Audit**: Collecting data on current access permissions for users across systems and applications.
   - **Validation**: Comparing current permissions with users' roles and responsibilities to identify discrepancies.
   - **Adjustment**: Modifying or revoking access as necessary to ensure compliance with access control policies.

4. **Implementation**: Access reviews can be managed manually or through automated tools within identity and access management (IAM) systems. Many IAM solutions offer built-in features for tracking, reporting, and adjusting access rights, making the review process more efficient and less prone to human error.

## **Case in Point**

In a hospital setting, an access review might involve evaluating the permissions of staff who have access to patient records and other sensitive information:

- **Staff Changes**: Review might reveal that a former employee still has access to the hospital’s EMR system. The review process would involve revoking this access to ensure that former staff members cannot access sensitive data.
- **Role Changes**: A nurse who has been promoted to a managerial role may need expanded access to certain systems for oversight purposes. The review process would adjust their access rights accordingly.
- **Compliance**: Regular access reviews ensure compliance with healthcare regulations like HIPAA by making sure that only authorized personnel can view and manage patient records.

By conducting regular access reviews, organizations maintain a tight grip on who can access their critical systems and data, thereby enhancing security and compliance.
