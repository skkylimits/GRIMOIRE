**Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)** refers to security solutions designed to monitor, detect, and respond to threats on endpoints such as computers, servers, and mobile devices. EDR solutions provide advanced capabilities for identifying suspicious activities, responding to incidents, and investigating security threats at the endpoint level.

## Key Features:

1. **Continuous Monitoring**:
   - **Real-Time Surveillance**: Continuously monitors endpoint activities to detect potential threats and suspicious behavior.

2. **Threat Detection**:
   - **Behavioral Analysis**: Uses behavioral analytics to identify anomalies and potential threats that may not be detected by traditional signature-based methods.
   - **Threat Intelligence Integration**: Incorporates threat intelligence to recognize known threats and emerging attack patterns.

3. **Incident Response**:
   - **Automated Response**: Offers automated responses to known threats, such as isolating infected endpoints or blocking malicious processes.
   - **Manual Response**: Provides tools for security teams to manually respond to and remediate incidents.

4. **Forensic Analysis**:
   - **Data Collection**: Collects and stores endpoint data for forensic analysis, including logs, system snapshots, and network activity.
   - **Investigation Tools**: Offers capabilities for investigating and analyzing security incidents, including timeline views and detailed activity reports.

5. **Threat Hunting**:
   - **Proactive Search**: Allows security analysts to proactively search for hidden threats and indicators of compromise (IOCs) across endpoints.
   - **Custom Queries**: Supports custom queries and searches to identify potential threats based on specific criteria.

6. **Integration**:
   - **SIEM Integration**: Integrates with Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems for comprehensive security monitoring and response.
   - **API Access**: Provides API access for integrating with other security tools and platforms.
