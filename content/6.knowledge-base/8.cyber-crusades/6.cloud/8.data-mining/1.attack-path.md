## **1️. Enumerate Directory Objects** 🧑‍💻👥
**Why?**
- Discover **users** and their associated metadata.
- Identify **groups** and see their memberships.
- Find **directory roles** like **Global Admin** and check who has them.
- Identify **devices** registered in the tenant and linked to users.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgUser -All
Get-MgGroup -All
Get-MgDirectoryRole
Get-MgDevice
```

## **2️. Identify Permissions & Access Control** 🔐
**Why?**
- Discover **app registrations** to see what apps are registered and what permissions they have.
- Enumerate **enterprise apps (service principals)** and their assigned permissions.
- Identify **group memberships** for privileged groups (Global Admins).
- Check for **OAuth2 permissions** that apps have been granted, which may allow unauthorized access to data.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgApplication
Get-MgServicePrincipal
Get-MgGroupMember -GroupId <groupId>
Get-MgOauth2PermissionGrant
```

## **3️. Explore Specific Access to Resources** 🏢📜
**Why?**
- Investigate what resources users have direct access to (e.g., **OneDrive**, **SharePoint**, **Teams**).
- Identify **active sessions** across devices for potential lateral movement opportunities.
- Understand **user-driven resources** that might have sensitive information or data exposure.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgUserDrive
Get-MgUserInsight
Get-MgUserManagedDevice
```

## **4️. Assess Roles & Privileges for Escalation** 🔓
**Why?**
- Check **role assignments** for users and service principals to identify high-privilege roles that can lead to escalation.
- Evaluate **directory role memberships** to see if users have admin or elevated permissions, such as Global Admin.
- Find if any **admin roles** have been granted broader access to critical resources or security data.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgRoleAssignment
Get-MgDirectoryRoleMember -DirectoryRoleId <roleId>
Get-MgDirectoryRoleMember -DirectoryRoleId <roleId>
```

## **5️. Identify Pivoting Opportunities** 💻⚙️
**Why?**
- Look for **enterprise apps** with broad permissions that can be leveraged to escalate privileges or pivot to other resources (e.g., access to **SharePoint**, **Exchange**, **Azure resources**).
- Investigate **access tokens** granted to apps which might allow for further exploitation.
- Find **privileged users** with access to critical assets, such as **Azure Storage** or **virtual machines**, for lateral movement or credential harvesting.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgServicePrincipal
Get-MgOauth2PermissionGrant
```

## **6️. Look for Data Exposure or Vulnerabilities** 📊
**Why?**
- Investigate **shared resources** (e.g., files on **OneDrive** or **SharePoint**) that might have been inadvertently exposed or shared with external users.
- Check for **sensitive data** that could be leveraged for **phishing** or **social engineering**.
- Look for **misconfigurations** in app permissions that could lead to unintentional data leaks.

**Commands:**
```powershell
Get-MgUserDriveSharedWithMe
```

This structured methodology allows attackers or security testers to systematically identify attack paths, uncover weaknesses in the environment, and find potential ways to escalate privileges or gain access to sensitive resources via **Microsoft Graph**.
