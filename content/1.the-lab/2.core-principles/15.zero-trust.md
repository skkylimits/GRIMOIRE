## **Guiding Principles**

> "trust no one, verify everything,"

Assuming all resources, even behind firewalls, are untrusted. Traditional security strategies are no longer enough, so Zero Trust strengthens security by verifying users and devices more rigorously.

## Zero Trust

* **Verify explicitly**: Always authenticate and authorize based on data points like user identity, location, device, service, and anomalies.
* **Least privileged access**: Limit access to the minimum necessary using adaptive, risk-based policies.
* **Assume breach**: Segment access, use encryption, and employ analytics to detect threats.

## **Six Foundational Pillars**

1. **Identities**: Verify all identities (users, services, devices) with strong authentication and least privilege access.
2. **Devices**: Monitor device health and compliance as they are a major attack surface.
3. **Applications**: Manage and monitor applications, including Shadow IT, to ensure proper access control.
4. **Data**: Classify, label, and encrypt data to ensure its security when leaving controlled environments.
5. **Infrastructure**: Assess infrastructure for vulnerabilities and use telemetry to detect and block risky behavior.
6. **Networks**: Segment networks and employ real-time threat protection, encryption, and monitoring.
