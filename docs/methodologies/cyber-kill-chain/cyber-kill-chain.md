# Cyber Kill Chain®

Developed by Lockheed Martin, the Cyber Kill Chain® framework is part of the Intelligence Driven Defense® model for identification and prevention of cyber intrusions activity.

The model identifies what the adversaries must complete in order to achieve their objective.

The seven steps of the Cyber Kill Chain® enhance visibility into an attack and enrich an analyst’s understanding of an adversary’s tactics, techniques and procedures.

Understanding the Cyber Kill Chain helps security teams take measures to prevent, detect, and stop attacks at various stages of the attack lifecycle.

## 1 Reconnaissance

The attacker gathers information about the target, such as IP addresses, server locations, and potential vulnerabilities.

## 2 Weaponization

Based on the gathered information, malicious programs or tools are created or modified to execute the attack.

## 3 Delivery

The malicious software or tools are delivered to the target system, usually through email attachments, phishing links, or other methods.

## 4 Exploitation

The attacker exploits weaknesses in the system to execute the malicious software and gain control over the target.

## 5 Installation

The malicious software is installed on the compromised system and begins executing the attacker's instructions.

## 6 Command and Control

The attacker establishes a communication channel with the infected systems to issue further instructions and steal data.
