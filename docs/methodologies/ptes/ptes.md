# Penetration Test Execution Standard

The Penetration Testing Execution Standard (PTES) provides a structured approach to conducting penetration tests. Here's how it works:

By following the PTES, organizations can receive an in-depth evaluation of their security measures and receive recommendations to enhance their protection against potential attacks.

## 1 Pre-engagement

The tester and the client define the scope, objectives, and limitations of the test. They also gather relevant information about the system or network to be tested.

## 2 Intelligence Gathering

The tester collects as much information as possible about the target system or network, including potential vulnerabilities, configurations, and possible attack surfaces.

## 3 Threat Modeling

The tester analyzes the gathered information to identify potential threats and risks, and to determine the most likely attack vector.

## 4 Vulnerability Analysis

The tester identifies and analyzes vulnerabilities in the target system or network, including known and potential weaknesses that could be exploited.

## 5 Exploitation

Based on the identified vulnerabilities, the tester performs targeted attacks to gain access to the system or network, gaining as much control as possible.

## 6 Post-Exploitation

After gaining access, the tester explores further opportunities, such as traversing the internal network, gathering sensitive information, or establishing persistent access.

## 7 Reporting

The tester documents all findings, including vulnerabilities, exploits, access levels, and recommendations for security improvements, and presents this to the client.
