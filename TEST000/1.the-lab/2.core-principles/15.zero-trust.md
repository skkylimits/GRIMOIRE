## **Guiding Principles**

> "trust no one, verify everything,"

Assuming all resources, even behind firewalls, are untrusted. Traditional security strategies are no longer enough, so Zero Trust strengthens security by verifying users and devices more rigorously.

## Zero Trust

* **Verify explicitly**: Always authenticate and authorize based on data points like user identity, location, device, service, and anomalies.
* **Least privileged access**: Limit access to the minimum necessary using adaptive, risk-based policies.
* **Assume breach**: Segment access, use encryption, and employ analytics to detect threats.

## **Six Foundational Pillars**

::caution
Each acts as a signal source, enforcement point, and resource to protect.
::

1. **Identities**: Verify all identities (users, services, devices) with strong authentication and least privilege access.
2. **Devices**: Monitor device health and compliance as they are a major attack surface.
3. **Applications**: Manage and monitor applications, including Shadow IT, to ensure proper access control.
4. **Data**: Classify, label, and encrypt data to ensure its security when leaving controlled environments.
5. **Infrastructure**: Assess infrastructure for vulnerabilities and use telemetry to detect and block risky behavior.
6. **Networks**: Segment networks and employ real-time threat protection, encryption, and monitoring.

## **Architekture**

Zero Trust is a full security strategy across identities, devices, data, apps, networks, and infrastructure.

![zero-trust-architekture](/content/1.the-lab/2.core-principles/15.zero-trust/zero-trust-architekture.png)

- Central focus on policy enforcement, including multifactor authentication with conditional access based on user risk and device health.
- Identities. Everything like people, devices, data, apps, and networks have security rules to keep them safe. For example, only healthy devices can use certain apps or see certain data.
- SIEM & XDR smart tools watch for dangers, find problems fast, and help fix them automatically.

::note
Security is like rules that protect everything in a company’s computer world. The most important part is making sure who you are (your identity) is checked carefully before you get in.
::

## Classic Approach

Once upon a time, companies kept their important data locked safely behind corporate networks. Only people connected to these networks could access what they needed. This worked well when everyone worked from the office.

![zero-trust-shift-986dd6f4-efd1c26c](/content/1.the-lab/2.core-principles/15.zero-trust/zero-trust-shift.png)

::caution
But then, things changed. People started working remotely, and businesses moved to the cloud. Suddenly, the old rules were too strict and slowed everyone down.
::

To solve this, companies began using a new way called Zero Trust. Instead of just guarding the network gates, Zero Trust says:

- Secure everything, everywhere: Protect assets no matter where they are — in the office, at home, or in the cloud.

- Focus on identity: Make sure the right people are who they say they are before giving access.

- Keep network security: Still use network protections, but not as the main way to control access.

- Stay flexible: Keep security strong but let employees work easily from anywhere.

And that’s how access control grew up and adapted to the modern world!
