**Intrusion Prevention System (IPS)** is a security technology designed to monitor network and system activities, detect potential threats, and take proactive measures to prevent these threats from causing harm. Unlike Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), which primarily focus on identifying and alerting on suspicious activities, IPS actively intervenes to block or mitigate threats in real-time.

## Key Components:

1. **Real-Time Monitoring**:
   - Continuously monitors network traffic and system activities for signs of malicious behavior or policy violations.

2. **Threat Detection**:
   - **Signature-Based Detection**: Uses known signatures of threats to identify malicious activities. Effective for known threats but limited in detecting new or modified attacks.
   - **Anomaly-Based Detection**: Detects deviations from established baseline behaviors, identifying potential threats that may not yet have known signatures.

3. **Automated Response**:
   - **Blocking**: Can automatically block malicious traffic or prevent harmful actions based on predefined rules and policies.
   - **Mitigation**: Takes immediate actions such as terminating connections, isolating affected systems, or applying security patches.

4. **Integration**:
   - **Security Coordination**: Often integrates with other security solutions, such as firewalls and SIEM systems, to provide a comprehensive defense mechanism.

5. **Logging and Reporting**:
   - **Detailed Records**: Logs detected threats and actions taken, providing valuable information for analysis, compliance, and improving security measures.
