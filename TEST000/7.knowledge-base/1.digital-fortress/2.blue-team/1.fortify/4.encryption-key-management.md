**Encryption key management** involves the creation, distribution, storage, and retirement of cryptographic keys used to protect sensitive data. Effective key management ensures that encryption keys are securely handled throughout their lifecycle to prevent unauthorized access and maintain data confidentiality and integrity.

## Key Management Processes:

1. **Key Generation**:
   - Creating strong cryptographic keys using secure algorithms to ensure that they are resistant to attacks.

2. **Key Distribution**:
   - Safely transmitting keys to authorized users or systems while protecting them from interception or misuse.

3. **Key Storage**:
   - Storing keys in a secure environment, such as a Hardware Security Module (HSM) or secure key vault, to prevent unauthorized access.

4. **Key Rotation**:
   - Regularly updating and replacing encryption keys to minimize the risk of compromise over time.

5. **Key Access Control**:
   - Implementing strict policies to control who can access or manage keys, ensuring that only authorized personnel can perform key-related operations.

6. **Key Disposal**:
   - Securely deleting keys that are no longer in use to prevent their potential recovery and misuse.

7. **Auditing and Monitoring**:
   - Keeping detailed logs and monitoring key usage to detect and respond to any unauthorized access or anomalies.
